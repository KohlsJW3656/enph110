Effects on the Human Body

Fatalities

  • Trapped in a fire, firefighters killed containing a blaze

  • Indirectly killed via smoke inhalation

  • Accidents while fleeing fires

Smoke

  • Wildfire smoke is made of fine particulate matter that can be harmful

  • Thought to kill 500 elderly people per year

  • Smoke can travel thousands of miles​

  • Wildfires contribute 25-50% of fine particles in the united states​

  • Evidence of harm to both lungs and heart​

Mental Health​

  • Following California's 2028 fire season, a study was conducted to examine the mental health impacts of wildfires​

  • Found that direct exposure to wildfires resulted in increased risk for PTSD and depression​

  • Households with an income greater or equal to $80,000 experienced less distress ​
